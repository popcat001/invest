# Portfolio Construction

## Portfolio Shape

Default portfolio design:

- Universe: AI-related public equities.
- Holdings: 8-15 positions.
- Core style: long-term structural ownership.
- Tactical overlay: used for entries, exits, trims, and position sizing.
- Review cadence: monthly plus event-driven reviews.

## Layer Allocation Ranges

Use the five-layer model as the main allocation map.

| Layer | Default range | Role |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| L1 Physical infrastructure | 10-20% | Defensive base and supply bottleneck exposure |
| L2 Digital infrastructure | 15-25% | Compute, cloud, data center, and networking exposure |
| L3 Core intelligence | 20-30% | Core AI model and platform exposure |
| L4 AI applications | 20-35% | Dynamic growth and revenue realization layer |
| L5 Frontier exploration | 0-10% | Optionality only |
| Cash | 5-20% | Dry powder, drawdown control, and event flexibility |

If the market is expensive or signal quality weakens, raise cash and reduce L5 and T4 exposure first. If high-quality L1-L3 assets become cheap during a broad selloff, use cash to add in stages.

## Position Sizing

| Position type | Initial size | Max size |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| T1 core | 4-8% | 15% |
| T2 growth engine | 3-6% | 10% |
| T3 theme allocation | 1-3% | 5% |
| T4 frontier option | 0.5-2% | 3% |
| Watchlist | 0% | 0% |

Sizing rules:

- Never start a full position at once. Use staged entries.
- Add only when both structural thesis and signal state support the action.
- Trim when position size exceeds the max, valuation becomes stretched, or signals deteriorate.
- Exit when the thesis breaks, not merely because price is volatile.

## Entry Rules

Before starting a position, require:

- Clear L layer assignment.
- Clear T tier assignment.
- Structural fit score of 19 or higher, unless explicitly marked as T4 optionality.
- Valuation note that explains why the current price is acceptable.
- Signal state of green or yellow, unless the action is a planned crisis entry.

Preferred entry pattern:

1. Starter position: 25-40% of intended size.
2. Add after confirmation: earnings, product traction, technical breakout, or valuation support.
3. Complete position only if the thesis strengthens and signal state remains green or yellow.

## Trim And Exit Rules

Trim when:

- The position exceeds its max size.
- The company moves from green to yellow and valuation is stretched.
- A better L/T opportunity appears with stronger signal quality.
- The position has become too correlated with another holding.

Exit when:

- The company loses structural relevance.
- The thesis depends mainly on narrative instead of measurable evidence.
- Signal state turns red and there is no near-term thesis repair path.
- Management credibility, balance sheet, or competitive position materially deteriorates.

## Rebalance Workflow

Monthly:

- Re-score each holding.
- Update L and T assignments.
- Check layer weights against target ranges.
- Check position sizes against max caps.
- Review signal colors and action list.

Event-driven:

- Re-score after earnings, major product launches, large contract wins/losses, major regulation, large price moves, or macro shocks.
- Act only if the event changes thesis, valuation, signal state, or risk.
