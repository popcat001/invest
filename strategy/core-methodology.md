# Core Methodology

## Investment Philosophy

The strategy looks for structural AI change, not daily noise. A structural change is a durable shift in cost curves, capability, demand, regulation, infrastructure, labor, or business models that can reshape industry profit pools over several years.

Daily news is useful only when it updates one of these questions:

- Does the company gain or lose a structural advantage?
- Does the opportunity move across layers or tiers?
- Does the expected return still justify the risk?
- Does the signal environment require a different entry, trim, or exit?

## L Dimension: Where The Money Goes

The L dimension answers: which layer of the AI economy receives capital, captures value, or becomes strategically necessary?

| Layer | Name | Role | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| L1 | Physical infrastructure | The physical base required for AI scale | Power, nuclear, grid, cooling, critical metals, fabs |
| L2 | Digital infrastructure | Compute and digital rails | AI chips, data centers, cloud, networking, storage |
| L3 | Core intelligence | Models, training systems, and AI operating engines | Foundation models, model labs, training clusters |
| L4 | AI applications | Revenue-producing AI use cases | Autonomous driving, robotics, AI software, AI drug discovery, defense |
| L5 | Frontier exploration | High-upside experimental frontiers | Space, quantum, advanced robotics, AI for science |

L4 is the dynamic residual layer. When L1, L2, and L3 are richly valued or crowded, L4 can absorb extra capital if applications show real revenue, unit economics, and customer adoption. When upstream layers are cheap and signal quality is strong, capital can rotate back toward the base layers.

## T Dimension: How To Operate

The T dimension answers: what tactical posture should be used for the position?

| Tier | Name | Default action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| T1 | Core pressure | Long-term core holding or accumulation candidate |
| T2 | Growth engine | Add in stages when trend and fundamentals confirm |
| T3 | Theme allocation | Smaller position tied to a theme or catalyst |
| T4 | Frontier option | Small, high-variance exposure or watchlist only |

T tiers are not business quality labels. They describe how aggressively to operate a position based on evidence, valuation, volatility, and signal quality.

## Structural Fit Score

Score each company from 1 to 5 in each category:

| Category | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Layer clarity | Which L layer does the company belong to, and is it obvious? |
| Value capture | Can the company capture economic value, not just create narrative value? |
| Data quality | Are revenue, margins, adoption, or capacity visible and measurable? |
| Moat | Does the company have scale, technology, distribution, supply, regulatory, or ecosystem advantage? |
| AI cycle health | Is the company benefiting from the current AI investment cycle without depending only on hype? |
| Valuation discipline | Does expected growth justify the price paid? |

Interpretation:

- 25-30: core candidate.
- 19-24: watch, stage in, or hold with defined triggers.
- 13-18: tactical or theme-only exposure.
- 12 or below: avoid unless there is a clearly priced asymmetric option.

## Noise Filter

Classify every new event before acting:

| Event type | Examples | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Noise | Commentary, short-term political headlines, daily price moves | Record only if it changes a signal |
| Catalyst | Earnings, product launch, contract, regulation, guidance | Re-score affected layers and tiers |
| Structural update | New cost curve, demand shock, competitive shift, infrastructure bottleneck | Rebuild thesis and position size |
| Thesis break | Lost moat, failed commercialization, permanent margin damage, fraud, liquidity stress | Exit or move to watchlist |

The default rule is: do not trade noise. Trade only signal changes, valuation dislocations, or thesis changes.
