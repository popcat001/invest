# Visual Breakdowns

Use this guide when creating a slide-like company breakdown similar to the Tesla and Google reference screenshots.

## Purpose

A visual breakdown is for fast judgment. It should answer:

- Where does the company sit in the L stack?
- Which T tier should govern position sizing?
- What is the company's strategic wedge?
- What is the current signal?
- What action follows?
- What trigger changes the decision?

It is not a research note. Put detailed evidence, sources, caveats, and long reasoning in a Markdown note.

## Content Budget

Default visual limits:

- One title.
- One subtitle or thesis sentence.
- Five L-layer rows or blocks.
- One T-tier/action panel.
- One strategic wedge, stated in one short phrase.
- No more than two short lines per layer.
- No more than five decision triggers.
- Avoid full sentences inside layer blocks unless necessary.

## Recommended Layouts

Use one of two layouts:

| Layout | Best for | Shape |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Five-layer stack | Companies dominated by one structural layer | L1-L5 horizontal blocks with weights or importance |
| L/T grid | Companies with meaningful exposure across tiers | Sparse grid with only high-signal cells filled |

For most company visuals, prefer the five-layer stack. Use the L/T grid only when the cross-layer/tier relationship is itself the point.

## What To Include

Each layer should include:

- Layer name.
- Weight or importance level.
- One business driver or dependency.

The action panel should include:

- T tier.
- Signal color.
- Suggested posture.
- Add/trim/exit triggers.

The visual must preserve the company's main wedge. Examples:

- AMD: CPU + GPU + rack-scale AI infrastructure.
- Tesla: AI + manufacturing + robotics/data flywheel.
- Google: distribution + data + model/application stack.

## What To Exclude

Keep these out of the visual unless they directly change the action:

- Long product lists.
- Full financial tables.
- Multiple partnership details.
- Every risk factor.
- Source URLs.
- Detailed valuation math.
- Speculative optionality with no near-term sizing impact.

## Quality Check

Before exporting a visual:

- A viewer should understand the main conclusion in under ten seconds.
- The largest visual emphasis should match the main thesis.
- No text should overflow or require zooming.
- The visual should still make sense without reading the research note.
